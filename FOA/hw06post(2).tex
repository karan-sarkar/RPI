\noindent{\bf Homework 6}

There are three homework problems. The expectation is that students
will submit very high quality proofs. The problem solutions must be written
in LaTeX and compiled to a pdf file. 
Follow the instructions on LMS for how to submit the problems.

\bigskip
\bigskip


\begin{enumerate}

\item Use the definition of convergent sequence to prove that
$\displaystyle \left( \frac{3+5n}{2n-4} \right)$ converges to $\displaystyle \frac{5}{2}.$ 
\item Use the definition of convergent sequence to prove that, for any real number $k>0$, 
$\displaystyle \left( \frac{1}{n^k} \right)$ converges to $0$.  
\item Suppose $s_1 = 2$ and $\displaystyle s_{n+1} = \frac{1}{6} \left(2s_n +5 \right)$ for $n \geq 1$.  
\begin{enumerate}
\item Prove that  $(s_n)$ is monotone 
\item Prove that $(s_n)$ is bounded.
\item Find $\lim s_n$.
\end{enumerate}
\vskip .1in



\end{enumerate}
